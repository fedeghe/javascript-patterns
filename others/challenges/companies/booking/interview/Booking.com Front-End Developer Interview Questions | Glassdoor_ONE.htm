Front-End Developer Interview (Positive Experience; Average Interview)

Front-End Developer
Moscow (Russia)

I applied online and the process took 2 weeks - interviewed at Booking.com in September 2013.

Interview Details – After sending my CV I received a JS task from booking's junior recruiter. Nothing special: to sort an array of objects, make a list of items and divide it into 3 columns. Add oncl1ck event to each item and put some data from item to a div. I was allowed to use any JS library. I used jQuery.

In a couple of days a got a mail that test was ok and they call me back next week. After a week of waiting i got another mail that my test was okay and that they'll call me back (again! same info but in other words:))

In a couple of days HR called me. We talked about my experience as a fronend dev. Why I want to work in booking.com? What is A/B testing?

The next day I had a phone interview with 2 guys from dev team. It took about 40-50 minutes. We talked about my daily workfrow at my current job (digital agency based in Moscow, Russia).
Then JS questions: scope, global variables, closures, event delegation (with practical example).
HTML+CSS questions: what is doctype, what happens if you don't provide it. Diff between display:block, display:inline, display:inline-block. Diff between position:absolute, position:relative. Other position values? What is the default one?
How to improve page loading speed?
Business questions: what is A/B testing? What are you going to measure on a booking confirmation page? On page next after confirmation page? I need to mention that I never used A/B testing, so I only knew some theory.
How to improve front-end part of booking.com? What i would do on my first working day?

Sometimes guys let me know if i'm answered correctly. Sometimes they just moved on to next question. I guess HR was also on the phone line because she checked her schedule for this tech interview.

In 3 days received letter that i failed. Here is a part from it:
At this time we regret to inform you that we cannot offer you an interview at our office.
Overall the interview went well. The interviewers were impressed with your JS knowledge and knowledge of optimization techniques. They however felt that you are missing the user focus and commercial awareness that is required for this role.
We will keep your resume and profile in our applicant database and encourage you to keep Booking.com in mind when considering future opportunities.

Interview Question – All questions about A/B testing. Just because of a lack of practice.   Answer Question

Front-End Developer Interview (Negative Experience; Average Interview)

Front-End Developer
Amsterdam (Netherlands)

I applied online and the process took 2 days - interviewed at Booking.com.

Interview Details – Two interviewers from the tech team called me the day after a first screening with the HR department. As for the previous reviews I read here, they talked about the team for 2-3 minutes, then quickly got started with technical questions on Javascript and font-end in general. Questions were quite easy and reasonable. You will not get a feeling from them on how you are doing, they won't spend a word to comment on your answers, which is brute, but I guess that's what HR tells them to do.

My answers on A/B testing questions probably didn't satisfy the team. I was asked about metrics, monitoring, strategies etc...from a commercial point of view, which is not my cup of tea as a software engineer. Fair enough.

Overall, during the phone call, didn't have the feeling that I was dealing with a strong engineering team, I might be wrong, but it felt like it's more about words than technologies.

Just check their website frontend code and...well...ouch.

The feedback that I received from the interview is that I did very well and impressed the team with the technical questions and the website review but they think my commercial awareness was not at the level that is required for this role.

I rate this experience negative because I have the feeling that the company is clearly not too keen in invest in people skills.

Front End Developer Interview (Positive Experience; Difficult Interview)

Anonymous Employee
Amsterdam (Netherlands)

I applied online and the process took 4 weeks - interviewed at Booking.com.

Interview Details – I applied online and was contacted within a week , a nice lady asked some basic questions about my willingness to relocate to Amsterdam and my experience and asked me to rate myself on a scale of 1- 10 on various skills like javsacript,jQuery,css,html. She informed about the company, their relocation package offer in short etc. and talked through the hiring process in detail. Asked me if I had any questions.

I was later told that I would have a telephonic interview and was kindly asked for a comfortable date.

Two guys from their front-end team interviewed me. They introduced themselves and the company, talked about their teams and the structure of the front-end team there. They started by asking about my experience and moved on to techincal questions. The interview lasted for about 45 mins. At the end they asked me if I had any questions.

The next day I was told I had cleared the telephonic and the team had almost no cons to report and were excited to meet me in Amsterdam for a face-to-face. The HR told me the company would pay for tickets,stay and discussed the visa process in detail, following it up with emails.

My visa process took a little longer and my face-to-face had to be postponed but the company was very supportive.

Finally the d-day came, went to Amsterdam, (awesome place btw ), the HR started by explaining in detail the relocation package ( which is pretty awesome if you ask me ) and asked me my motivation for moving, what I could bring to booking and any hesitations I had.

Then followed the two hours of technical interviews in detail. First a pair of front-end devs who seem to have the same experience as me interviewed followed by a pair of guys who seem to be leads in front-end teams. Both interviews were purely techincal with the latter having some non-technical questions.

 At the end the front-end manager talked to me for an hour asking in detail about my previous company ( I think he had some experience in that domain so he got curious about my company) , he told me what to expect at Booking and explained the Priceline groups structure in detail.

Unfortunately, the process ended here for me. I got positive feedback in all skills except native javascript. I was told I was unsuccessful and they would keep my resume on file for 6 months for future.

I do hope they call me back :P

Interview Question – Telephonic : What is a doctype ? What happens if we dont specify one.
Ways to improve performance ?
Diff between display:block and inline, what are closures with example.
Suggestions for website.

Face-to-Face:
Asked me to design a menu, first with only css, then using JS. Not end-to-end code but rought approach was asked. They asked questions as I discussed my solution. Be prepared to have clarity in your approach as the interview will steer in a direction depending upon what you say. ( they don't really ask a list of hit-or-miss questions)

Was asked about one challenging thing I had to do in my company which directly benefited business.   Answer Question

I applied online and the process took 2 weeks - interviewed at Booking.com.

Interview Details – Applied through linkedIn, got an email saying they want to do a follow up over the phone.
after three or four days someone from HR contacted me, we talked for about 20 minutes about my experience and my knowledge in different technologies as well about my willingness to relocate to Amsterdam.

At the end she said she would like send me a task to preform and send back the solution.
it was pretty basic, getting a Javascript array, sorting it and displaying it in HTML, clicking on a link would retrieve more info from the array.
i got an answer a day later that it was great and they would like to schedule a technical interview with two developers.

The interview started with questions about my background, and quickly moved to technical questions, it took about 40 minutes.
I was also required to give suggestions on how i can think their site can be improved.
They give you zero feedback about your answers, and they are pretty robotic.

sadly i got a negative response 2 days later.

If someone would have bothered to write more questions that he was asked maybe i could have prepared better!!!!!!
